The Human Crisis

For many children in South Africa, the daily commute to school is a life-threatening journey. Forced to cross dangerous bodies of water without basic swimming education, these students risk drowning simply to get an education. We aim to shed light on this issue to bring vital swimming lessons and safety resources to the communities that need them most.

The Conservation Crisis

The illegal wildlife trade has ignited a brutal poaching epidemic across South Africa, bringing rhinos to the brink of extinction. Due to the demand for their horns, poachers leave behind a trail of devastated ecosystems and orphaned calves. We want to document this war, raise awareness about the harsh reality and support the anti-poaching units fighting to protect these animals.

Our Vision & Mission

Long Swim To Freedom is more than a documentary — it is a call to action at the intersection of two preventable tragedies: children drowning on the way to school, and rhinos vanishing due to poaching. We believe awareness, properly channelled, saves lives.

Vision

A South Africa where no child risks their life to reach school, and where the rhino is no longer a target. We envision thriving communities and ecosystems, protected through education, awareness, and direct action.

Mission

To produce a powerful documentary that exposes these dual crises, raises critical funds for anti-poaching units and water safety programmes, and mobilises a global audience to become agents of change.

Objectives

Deliver certified swimming lessons to 500+ children in high-risk areas. Fund and equip frontline ranger units in iSimangaliso. Release the documentary to international audiences and drive measurable policy engagement.

Target Audience

Conservation advocates, humanitarian donors, film festival communities, impact investors, and everyday global citizens who believe storytelling can be a catalyst for real-world change.

Expected Outcomes

Reduced child drowning incidents in target communities. Increased ranger capacity and equipment. International distribution of the film reaching over 1 million viewers. Sustainable local swimming and safety programmes.

Why Now

Rhino poaching surged again in 2024. Drowning remains the second leading cause of unintentional injury death for South African children. The window to document and act is closing — this film must be made today.

The Impact We're Creating

Long Swim To Freedom is designed to generate lasting, measurable change — on screen and off it. Every frame shot, every lesson taught, and every ranger equipped moves the needle on three interconnected frontiers.

Youth & Conservation

By embedding local youth in the conservation story — as participants, not bystanders — we build the next generation of environmental stewards. Children who learn to swim safely are also introduced to the waterways and wildlife they will one day protect.

Climate & Ecosystem Awareness

The wetlands and reserves of iSimangaliso are on the frontline of climate change. Our documentary brings this reality to global audiences, connecting the dots between environmental degradation, wildlife loss, and human vulnerability.

Community Transformation

From funding safe river crossings to establishing permanent swimming programmes, the project leaves behind infrastructure and skills that outlast the film itself. Communities become partners, not just subjects.

Where Your Support Goes

To make Long Swim To Freedom a reality and create lasting change, we need your help. Your funding directly supports our on-the-ground efforts in South Africa, from the documentary production to immediate community and conservation interventions.

Water Safety

Funding certified swimming instructors, safe pool access, and essential water safety gear for local children.

Anti-Poaching Support

Equipping frontline rangers with vital survival gear, medical supplies, and technological support to protect the rhinos.

Film Production

Covering camera gear, crew logistics, and post-production costs to bring this vital documentary to life.

Community Outreach

Working directly alongside community leaders to build sustainable educational programs and safe transport alternatives.

Logistics & Transport

Navigating the challenging South African terrain safely for both our documentary crew and the communities we assist.

Global Impact

Marketing and distribution of the finished documentary to maximize worldwide awareness and drive policy change.
